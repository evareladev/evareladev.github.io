    Development of a system for analyzing river flow velocities from aerial images for use in decision support systems for flood mitigation (In Spanish)

    Published in Congreso de Ingeniería y Arquitectura - CONIA 2019, 2020

    The research presented in this article proposes the development of an application for calculating the average flow velocity of a river, aimed at flood risk management in vulnerable areas—a problem that has affected El Salvador in recent years. To implement this proposal, a Python program has been developed that uses the YOLO algorithm to identify objects within video frames, as well as a tracking algorithm based on the centroid method, which captures the central point of the identified object and stores a record of recognized objects throughout the video. With this record, it is possible to calculate the average speed of each identified object using established mathematical methods, including the conversion of pixels to meters in the obtained result. All this information can be clearly and accurately displayed through a graphical interface, allowing the end user to visualize the results of the calculations performed on a provided input video.

    Recommended citation: Varela, E., Callejas, M., López, A., Rivera, C., & Vidal, N. (2020). Desarrollo de sistema de análisis de velocidades de flujo en ríos a partir de imágenes aéreas para su utilización en sistemas de soporte a la decisión para mitigación de inundaciones. CONIA, Congreso de Ingeniería y Arquitectura 2019, 1, 33–36. http://repositorio.uca.edu.sv/jspui/handle/11674/3806
